Who We Are

Like the Sherpas who help Everest climbers during their gruelling quest by deep knowledge and understanding of terrain, routes and weather conditions that maximises the chances of reaching the summit, our coaches advise, enable and assist our clients who are on a mission to reach higher levels of productivity in difficult environments with limited options.

The teams and organizations we work with, just like the Everest expeditions, must put in the effort, endure the hardships and face the risks involved in reaching their goal. Our job is to assist them on this journey for maximum results.

In order to achieve higher level of productivity, our clients aim to sustainably and quickly deliver value and innovation by creating learning organizations. With our deep knowledge in lean and agile thinking and organizational tools we provide guidance and support to over-achieve these goals.

How We Work

We have a comprehensive and proven approach to guide organizations and individuals to achieve three results: stronger performance, more productive working relationships, and greater individual well-being.

  • System

    Our coaching approach is rested on two pillars: Systems Thinking and Servant Leadership. Systems thinking is a conceptual framework, a body of knowledge and a set of thinking tools to help us see system dynamics, see mental models and see local optimization.

  • Mindset

    Instead of seeing problems as caused by someone or something “out there” and reacting to problems it helps see the bigger picture and how our own actions create the problems we experience. This creates a shift of mind that is at the core of a learning organization.

  • Skills

    We believe that Servant Leadership in combination with Skilled Facilitation is key to introducing a new way of thinking and operating in the enterprise. Through our coaching, we empower both formal and informal leaders so that, through their behavior, they help a team evolve into a performing unit that meets the expectations of its clients, becomes better over time, and contributes positively to the personal learning and well-being of its member.

  • Initiatives

    They do this by creating and maintaining the conditions that allow them to move into that direction, like the clarity of direction, a good structure, an enabling context, and competent coaching. On top of that, they are applying a compassionate method from more effective team facilitation based on the Mutual Learning approach.
